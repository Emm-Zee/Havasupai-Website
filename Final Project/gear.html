People spend a lot of time considering what they will and will not need for a multi-day backpacking trip. If your trip lasts longer than a week, you will want to organize food drop off/pick-up points to replenish your supply. You'll also want to map areas along your trip where you can refill your water.

You have a couple of options for transporting your gear. You can mail your backpack to the village if you don’t want to fly with it. For an additional fee, you can have horses and pack mules carry your gear down while you bring a day pack for your hike though animal rights activists (and backpacking enthusiasts) challenge the ethics of this practice. There are numerous Outfitters throughout Arizona that you can rent gear from as well (which is what we did for our backpacks, tent, sleeping bags, sleeping bag, and cooking stove). Aside from that, if you are flying in or coming from across the country, I reccommend purchasing your food while you're in Arizona. Depending on which direction you are coming from, you can stop at a town a few hours away and stock up. We passed through Flagstaff and were able to get everything we needed from REI and Wal-Mart.
Essentials

  • Backpack - make sure to get one that is comfortable and fits your frame
  • Tent or hammock
  • Sleeping bag
  • Sleeping pad


Food

Tip: Calculate food based on caloric needs per day and multiple by the number of days visiting

  • Portable stove

  • Hydration pack
  • Water filtration system


Clothing
