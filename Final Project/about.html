Havasupai Falls is a destination for hikers and outdoor enthusiasts. Nestled at the bottom of the Grand Canyon, Havasu Creek flows down a series of falls that leads into the Colorado River. The village of Supai is nestled along Havasu Creek and is considered the most remote village in the country; it is only accessible by foot, helicopter, horse or mule. The pony express is still utilized here as you witness goods, Amazon boxes and USPS packages carried into town. Roughly 300 villagers live in Supai. Most work to support the tourism as visitors flow throughout the tribal grounds throughout the year.
